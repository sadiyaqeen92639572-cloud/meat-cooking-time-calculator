Multiply the minutes per pound for your meat and doneness by the weight, then add the base offset. For example, medium roast beef is about 23 minutes per pound plus 25 minutes, so a 4 lb joint takes roughly 1 hr 56 min at 350°F. Always confirm with a meat thermometer.
Beef and lamb: 125°F rare, 140°F medium, 158°F well done. Pork: 160°F. Chicken: 165°F. Ham/gammon: 154°F. Measure the thickest part with a meat thermometer.
Yes. A convection (fan) oven runs about 25–35°F hotter than a conventional oven, so set it lower for the same cooking time. This calculator lets you choose fan or conventional.
Rest small cuts 8–10 minutes and large roasts 15–30 minutes, loosely tented with foil. Resting lets the juices redistribute so the meat stays moist when carved.
